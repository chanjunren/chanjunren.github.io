# jumping_in_slalom

> In slalom, jumps are about **glide**, not height. Jump small, land quiet, keep shoulders open.

## Counter-intuitive principle

Slalom jumps ≠ athletic jumps. The goal is **minimal vertical displacement** with maximum sliding saturation.

## Why jump small

- **Center of gravity**: the higher you jump, the more your center of gravity fluctuates
- Fluctuation → imbalance → secondary corrective forces on landing → broken flow
- Lower jumps = **smoother transitions** back into gliding

## Key techniques

### Shoulder balance
- **Open arms** and spread shoulders during jumps — this is critical
- Shoulders maintain balance during airborne phase
- Without open shoulders, the body rotates uncontrollably mid-air

### Land quiet
- Test: listen to wheel-to-ground contact sound
- Ideal: **barely audible** landing
- Use **knees and ankles** as shock absorbers on landing
- "Slide into" the landing rather than "drop onto" it

### Maximize sliding trajectory
- Jump at the **extreme position** of inner/outer-eight stance — where the move naturally needs a transition
- Example: X-jump — jump when feet are at maximum inner/outer-eight spread
- Keep the jump phase as **short as possible**, get back to sliding quickly

### Use 磨力换点 (friction-point switching) early on
- Before attempting real jumps, practice the same transitions using ground friction instead of air
- Builds the feeling of weight transfer without the instability

## X-jump example

- X trajectory = inner/outer-eight transitions
- Jump point = when feet reach extreme outer-eight spread
- Jumping = transitioning faster to the next inner/outer-eight state
- The "jump" is really just a **quick lift** to switch states
