# brush

> Brush (刷子) — a scissor-class (剪类) move where one foot traces an S-curve while the other traces a Z-line through the cones.

## Trajectory

- **S foot**: weaves around cones in an S-curve
- **Z foot**: moves in a straight zigzag line
- Combined: creates a scissor-like crossing pattern

## Step-by-step (forward brush)

1. **Start**: feet parallel outer-eight, body facing away from cone line
2. Do half a **back scissors** to cross past the cone — maintain crossed outer-eight between two cones
3. **Slight squat** at knees → use **waist/hip force system** → rotate ankles ~45-90 degrees
4. Lower body follows upper body's rotation (involves body turning)
5. **Open** the crossed legs to pass the next cone — feet open shoulder-width, landing on both sides of the cone
6. **End position** = starting position (outer-eight parallel) → can repeat infinitely

## Key points

- **Waist/hip initiation**, not ankle-driven — the hip force system (腰胯发力体系) powers the crossing
- Opening width = shoulder width, no wider
- The body turn in step 4 is what distinguishes brush from a simple scissors

## Variants

- **Forward brush** and **reverse brush** — same principle, different facing direction
- **点规 Brush**: five-wheel (toe) state throughout — the most commonly seen form
- Front brush is harder — requires significant ligament flexibility

Brush is highly extensible via [[movement_extension_method]]:
- Element addition (add pivot → pivot brush)

- Deconstruction and recombination
