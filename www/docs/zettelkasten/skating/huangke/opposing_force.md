# opposing_force

> 对向发力 — pushing legs apart then pulling together creates visible speed bursts and explosive contrast.

## How it differs from relative force

- **[[relative_force]]** = front/back opposing motion for smoothness
- **Opposing force** = open/close (lateral or longitudinal) for **speed contrast**
- The visual punch comes from the **transition between spread and gathered** states

## The open-close cycle

1. **Open** (开): legs push apart — thigh outer muscles drive outward
2. **Close** (合): legs pull together — thigh inner muscles pull inward
3. Repeat — each cycle generates a visible speed burst

### Lateral feel
- Imagine pushing both legs outward sideways, then snapping them back together
- Like doing fast alternating crossovers without actually crossing

### Longitudinal feel
- Legs alternating front/back rapidly — like quick scissors
- Reference: fast alternating sliding drill

## How to see the effect

Same combo, two ways:
1. Without opposing force — smooth but flat speed
2. With opposing force — visible **acceleration/deceleration contrast** that reads as explosive

The speed change creates visual impact even when actual distance covered is similar.

## Where it applies

Any move with a **crossing or spreading** component:
- 前剪 (front scissors), Crazy, X, 潘腾 (Panteng), Italian
- 点规 (pivot) transitions — push apart during arc, pull together at pivot switch
- Especially effective in 即兴 (improvisation) for musical emphasis
