# volt_pivot

> 点规 (pivot/compass) — one foot stays on a cone as the pivot point while the other foot arcs around it, like a compass drawing a circle.

## 点规 as a move class

点规 is both a **specific move** and a **movement category**:
- As a class: any move where one foot is the pivot (圆心) and the other draws an arc
- Most common basic move in this class: **Volt**

## Volt step-by-step

### Step 1 — Setup
- Outer-eight stance, right foot near cone line
- Pull left foot back to first cone, bend knees, lower center of gravity
- Fix the pivot foot position

### Step 2 — Forward pivot (正点规)
- **Shoulder drives** the right foot through an arc
- Skating foot arcs to upper side of two-cone gap
- Body rotates 180 degrees
- Keep shoulders balanced, minimize secondary adjustments mid-rotation

### Step 3 — Switch pivot foot
- Between cones 1 and 2, swap which foot is the pivot
- Feet form an **isosceles triangle** relative to the cone line
- Pull new pivot foot back to cone 3

### Step 4 — Reverse pivot (倒点规)
- Arc the skating foot again, rotating 180 degrees back to starting orientation
- Bring feet together → return to starting position
- Repeatable infinitely

## Four-direction pivots (四向点规)

Pivots have four states based on foot alignment:
1. **Inner-eight parallel** pivot
2. **Outer-eight parallel** pivot
3. **Cross inner-eight** pivot
4. **Cross outer-eight** pivot (hardest — feels anti-human)

Practice all four in sequence for complete pivot mastery.

## Key details

- Skating foot primarily uses **outer edge** (外刃) during the arc
- The arc foot trajectory = front/back parallel inner/outer-eight + front/back crossed inner/outer-eight
