# banana_rocker_setup

> Banana rocker = wheel height difference from center to ends. 2mm is standard, 4mm is "big banana" — more agile but harder to control.

## What the rocker does

- **Wheel drop** creates an arc (like a banana's curve) under the foot
- More drop → **faster turning response**, less effort to change direction
- Less drop → **more stability**, better straight-line performance

## Standard (2mm) — 普通香蕉

- Default on most skates — neither pure slalom nor pure speed
- Two wheels slightly smaller in the center create 2mm height difference
- Balanced between turning agility and straight-line stability

## Big banana (4mm) — 大香蕉

- Used by serious slalom skaters
- **Advantages**: much easier turning, less effort on reversals, saves energy
- **Disadvantages**: very unstable, requires long adaptation period, demands strong balance
- Transition from 2mm → 4mm takes weeks of dedicated practice

## The extended-frame trick

To get 4mm agility with more stability:
- Keep 4mm wheel drop, but use a **longer frame**
- 219mm frame → swap to 231mm
- 231mm frame → swap to 243mm
- Longer frame = more stability, offsetting the instability of big banana
- Keep wheel sizes the same to maintain consistent ride height and center of gravity

## Alternative: big-banana frame + reverse-banana wheels

- 2mm wheel drop (stable) on a big-banana frame design
- Frame height itself adds turning responsiveness
- Front/back larger wheels improve single-wheel experience
- Compensates for the poor single-wheel feel of standard big banana

See also [[asymmetric_rocker]] for a more nuanced approach.

## Context: why slalom needs more rocker than speed

- Speed slalom / 刹车: uses **flat or reverse banana** — sacrifices turning for maximum ground contact and acceleration
- 花桩: needs to turn constantly in 80cm cone spacing — more rocker = less effort per turn = longer sessions
