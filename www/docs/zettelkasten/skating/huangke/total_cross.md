# total_cross

> 翻刃 — a body-roll (卷体类) movement where the upper body initiates rotation, transmitting force down through the hips to the feet.

## Core mechanic

**Upper body drives lower body** — the opposite of most slalom moves:
1. **Shoulder** initiates the rotation
2. Force transmits through **waist/hips** to lower body
3. **Ankles rotate** simultaneously, feet press onto edges
4. Feet cross in **outer-eight support** while in an inner-eight visual position

## Step-by-step (forward 翻刃)

1. Enter with feet **parallel, open** — starting position
2. Slightly rotate upper body (shoulder-led)
3. Turn ankles, both feet tilt onto edges — angle ~45-90 degrees toward the crossing direction
4. Cross legs, passing the cone — feet are crossed outer-eight but **supported on inner-eight edges** (press outer edge)
5. Open the crossed position — end in inner-eight stance
6. Body has rotated **180 degrees** from start to finish

## Varieties

- **Forward-to-back** and **back-to-forward** — distinguished by body facing direction
- **点规翻刃** (pivot total cross): same movement but in 5-wheel (toe) stance — increases sliding distance significantly
- Raise the crossing point higher for more advanced execution

## Practice value

- Trains **anti-dizziness** and rotational awareness
- Appears in many combos as a transition element
- Foundation for body-roll class movements
- Extensible via element addition, substitution, and [[movement_extension_method]]
