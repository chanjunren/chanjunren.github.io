# asymmetric_rocker

> Non-symmetric banana rocker: front wheels at 2mm drop, rear wheels at 4mm drop. Stability where you need it, agility where it matters.

## The insight

- Slalom skates (especially 平花鞋) have a **high heel** — weight naturally shifts to the forefoot
- Forefoot = where stability matters most → keep standard **2mm drop** here
- Rear = closest to the **ankle pivot point** → use **4mm big banana** for turning agility
- Effective rocker = ~3mm average (between 2mm and 4mm)

## Why it works

### Forefoot stability
- High-heel slalom boots put your center of gravity over the front wheels
- 2mm drop at the front stabilizes your primary weight-bearing point

### Rear agility
- The ankle is the main turning joint
- 4mm drop at the rear sits closest to this pivot
- When you rotate your ankle or shift weight, the rear 4mm responds **immediately**
- Result: turning feels natural and effortless — "自然丝滑轻松"

### Single-wheel benefit
- Front and back wheels are larger than center wheels
- Better single-wheel experience than symmetric big banana
- More ground contact on single-wheel → more stable, less likely to drop

## Wheel configuration example

For a 231mm frame:
- Front two wheels: 84mm (2mm drop between them)
- Rear two wheels: 84mm front / 76mm rear (4mm drop)
- Center wheels smaller in both pairs

## Who it's for

- **Improvisation skaters** and advanced players
- Not beginner-friendly — the asymmetry feels weird at first
- Best for those already comfortable with standard banana who want more turning response
