# trick_slalom

> Trick Slalom — a Japanese tradition using large cone spacing (1.5-2m) that makes slalom more accessible for beginners and more expressive for advanced skaters.

## What it is

- **Large cones, large equipment** — cone spacing 1.5m to 2m (vs. standard 80cm)
- Originated in **Japan** as a traditional slalom format
- Bigger spacing → longer sliding trajectories → richer route possibilities

## Why it's better for beginners

Standard 80cm slalom is brutal for new skaters:
- Sub-1-meter spacing → most beginners **hit cones** rather than weave around them
- Tight space → body coordination breaks down → stiff, uncoordinated movements
- Upper body rigidity and balance problems are amplified

With 1.5-2m spacing:
- More **room to maneuver** — you can actually arc around cones instead of squeezing past
- Coordination develops naturally because there's space to adjust
- Builds 绕桩 (weaving) ability instead of 闭桩 (cone-dodging)

## Why it's great for advanced skaters

- Longer runs between cones = **faster skating, bigger moves**
- More space for **explosive amplitude** — moves look more powerful
- Extended sliding arcs develop skating ability beyond what tight cones allow
- Creative freedom: more room = more route options per cone pair

## The logic

- If you can handle large cones easily, small cones become **trivially easier**
- "大桩功能轻松拿捏之后，小桩岂不是手到擒来"
- It's a training progression: large → standard, not the other way around

## Getting started

- Use simple 平花 moves initially — no need for advanced tricks
- The large spacing itself is the training stimulus
- Equipment: any standard slalom setup works — the cones change, not the skates
