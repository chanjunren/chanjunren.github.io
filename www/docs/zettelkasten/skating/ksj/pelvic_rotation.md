# pelvic_rotation

**Pelvic utilization** is the conscious engagement of the pelvis to generate power and efficiency in slalom skating — especially one-foot techniques like backward heel wheeling. It replaces reliance on raw leg muscle force, which causes fatigue and plateaus.

## Internal vs External Focus

Two ways to think about movement:

- **External focus** — the visible outcome (e.g. "arm raised to shoulder height")
- **Internal focus** — the body mechanics producing it (e.g. "shoulder abducted to shoulder line")

External focus drives early improvement. At advanced levels, **internal focus on pelvic mechanics becomes essential** — external cues alone are insufficient.

## Why the Pelvis

- Leg-force-only approach plateaus hard (~20 cones) and causes fatigue
- Shifting internal focus to the pelvis unlocks exponential gains (40 → 80 → 100+ cones)
- Applies across most one-foot slalom techniques, not just backward wheeling
- Often partially present in a skater's movement before they consciously realise it
