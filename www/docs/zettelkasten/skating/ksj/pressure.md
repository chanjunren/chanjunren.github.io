# pressure

**Pressure** is the intentional push force that generates speed. Without it you can only coast on existing momentum or slope.

It's the third layer in the foundation: posture → [[practise_slowly|compression]] → pressure.

## Pressure Needs Compression

Pressure without compression doesn't transfer — the force has nowhere to go.

Analogy: a car wheel spinning in the air. The engine works but the car doesn't move. Compression puts the wheel on the ground.

This is why beginners fatigue fast: they apply pressure without compression, so they push harder and more often for the same result.

## Supporting Concepts

- **Balance area** — the minimum footprint keeping you stable; shifts dynamically as you apply pressure and gain speed (static balance ≠ dynamic balance)
- **Edge** — the angled contact between skate and ground during a push; naturally engages when pressure is applied correctly; makes pushes and braking efficient
- **Weight shift** — transferring load from one leg to the other mid-push; accounts for over 70% of beginner problems

```ad-warning
"Give strength" is too vague to be useful as a cue. Pressure is purposeful: apply it *through* proper compression, not instead of it.
```
