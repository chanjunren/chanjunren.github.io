# pressure

**Pressure** is the push force that creates speed. Without it you can only coast on existing momentum.

It's the third layer in the foundation: posture → [compression](practise_slowly.md) → pressure.

## Pressure Needs Compression

Pressure without compression doesn't transfer — the force has nowhere to go.

Analogy: a car wheel spinning in the air. The engine works but the car doesn't move. Compression puts the wheel on the ground.

This is why beginners tire quickly: they apply pressure without compression, so they push harder and more often for the same result.

## Supporting Concepts

- **Balance area** — the smallest contact area keeping you stable; changes as you move and gain speed
- **Edge** — the angled contact between skate and ground; kicks in naturally with proper pressure; makes pushes and braking more efficient
- **Weight shift** — shifting weight from one leg to the other mid-push; accounts for over 70% of beginner problems

```ad-warning
"Give strength" is too vague to act on. Pressure is specific: apply it *through* proper compression, not instead of it.
```
