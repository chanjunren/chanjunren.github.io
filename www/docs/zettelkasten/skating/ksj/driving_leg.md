# driving_leg

The **driving leg** is whichever leg carries more than 50% of your body weight at a given moment — it's the leg doing the actual work of moving you forward.

This matters because skaters often push with the wrong leg at the wrong time without noticing, leading to fatigue and choppy transitions.

## Three Leg Roles (Don't Confuse Them)

| Role | What it does |
|------|-------------|
| **Driving leg** | >50% body weight; generates forward propulsion |
| **Supporting leg** | Provides balance via compression; doesn't glide |
| **Axis / pivot leg** | The leg rotation happens *around* |

Mixing these up leads to misdiagnosing technique problems.

## How the Driving Leg Switches

In a standard 8-step movement cycle:

- Steps 1–4: right leg drives
- Steps 4–5: transition — driving leg switches (in sync with weight shift)
- Steps 5–8: left leg drives

When the switch is mistimed, you compensate by pushing with the *back* leg instead of transferring weight forward — like walking upstairs by always pushing up with the same foot.

## Common Error

Skaters unknowingly keep driving off the hind leg rather than letting the switch happen. This causes:
- Faster fatigue
- Choppy transitions (breaks [linkage](crazy.md))
- Deeply ingrained habit that's hard to correct later

Most visible in Crazy and bolt movements.

## Spotting It When Observing

In a zigzag, the driving leg is always the *opposite* leg from the one doing the heel/toe hold.

Use this as a quick diagnostic when watching someone else skate.
