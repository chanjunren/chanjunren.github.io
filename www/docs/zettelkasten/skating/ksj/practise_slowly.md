# practise_slowly

"Practice slowly" doesn't mean move slowly forever — it means **build compression before adding speed**.

Beginners trying to match the pace of advanced skaters skip the part that makes those skaters effective: getting their weight properly grounded first.

## The Core Chain

**Compression** → enables **pressure** → generates **speed**

- **Compression** — your full body weight is settled onto the grounded foot; it feels planted and stable
- **Pressure** — the push force that creates speed; only transfers if compression is solid first
- **Leaning** — tilting your body while compressed; causes a **centroid shift** (centre of mass moves forward), which generates initial movement *without* needing to push

```ad-example
Compression is like grip. A sprinter pushes *through* the ground, not just *at* it. Compression is what establishes that grip.
```

## Why Beginners Should Go Slow

At speed, beginners default to pushing harder — but without compression the force doesn't transfer. Slowing down gives you time to feel and hold compression before moving.

Once compression is automatic, speed builds naturally through proper pressure. Skipping this puts a hard ceiling on how far your technique can go.
