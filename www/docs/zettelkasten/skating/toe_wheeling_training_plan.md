# toe_wheeling_training_plan

> 12-week [[periodization]] plan to break the toe wheeling plateau

## the problem

Progressed from 5x2min to 4x2min30s wall-supported holds, then stalled. Same stimulus → accommodation → no further adaptation. Need to rotate training focus.

## intensity lever

| Variant | Intensity | Use |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Wall-supported hold | Low-moderate | Endurance work, deload |
| Unsupported hold (no wall) | High | Strength and power work |
| Unsupported hold → immediate cone work | Max | Power / transfer work |

## mesocycle 1 — strength (weeks 1-4)

**Goal**: Build max single-leg isometric strength on toe wheels

**Primary sessions (2-3x/week)**:
- 5-6 x 45s-1min unsupported holds
- Last 15s of each hold should be a fight to maintain
- Rest 2-3min between holds (full recovery for quality)
- Progress: Week 1 → 5x45s, Week 2 → 5x50s, Week 3 → 6x50s

**Maintenance (1x/week)**:
- 2 x 2min supported holds (keep endurance from regressing)

**Week 4 — Deload**:
- 3 x 45s at ~80% effort
- Focus on form: toe height, no knee bend, weight along cone line not on ankle

## mesocycle 2 — power / dynamic (weeks 5-8)

**Goal**: Translate static strength into dynamic wheeling quality

**Primary sessions (2-3x/week)**:
- 6 x 30s max effort unsupported holds
- After each hold, immediately transition to wheeling through 10 cones
- The transition is the training — carrying max-effort fatigue into skill execution
- Progress: increase cone count or decrease spacing each week

**Maintenance (1x/week)**:
- 3 x 45s unsupported holds (keep strength)

**Week 8 — Deload**:
- 3 x 30s at ~80% + casual cone work

## mesocycle 3 — endurance / application (weeks 9-12)

**Goal**: Extend duration on a stronger base; integrate into routine practice

**Primary sessions (2-3x/week)**:
- 4 x 3min+ supported holds (should feel easier than old 2:30 — you're stronger now)
- 1 x max unsupported hold per session (test your new ceiling)
- Progress: add 15-30s per hold each week

**Application (1-2x/week)**:
- Routine sections requiring sustained toe wheeling
- Choreo practice with [[combo]] difficulty chains

**Week 12 — Deload + Retest**:
- Retest: max supported hold, max unsupported hold
- Compare to pre-plan baseline
- Decide next macrocycle focus

## weekly template

| Day | Mesocycle 1 | Mesocycle 2 | Mesocycle 3 |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Mon | Strength (unsupported) | Power (hold → cones) | Endurance (supported) |
| Tue | Cone work / routine | Cone work / routine | Cone work / routine |
| Wed | Strength (unsupported) | Power (hold → cones) | Endurance (supported) |
| Thu | Rest or light skate | Rest or light skate | Rest or light skate |
| Fri | Endurance maintenance | Strength maintenance | Application / choreo |
| Sat | Strength (unsupported) | Power (hold → cones) | Max unsupported test |

Adjust to your actual 4-5 day schedule. The principle: 2-3 days primary focus, 1 day maintenance, rest is application.

## bob bowman integration

> [[bob_bowman_golden_rules]]

**Dream**: Smooth, sustained toe wheeling in choreographed routines with clean difficulty chains

**Short range**: Complete this 12-week periodized block

**Immediate (WIN)**:
Each session has ONE focus — strength OR power OR endurance. Never mixed. That's your "What's Important Now."

```ad-quote
"Practice getting the best possible performance in the worst possible conditions"
```

Unsupported holds = your worst condition. That's where the growth is.

```ad-quote
"High performance is a natural outcome of all your small decisions"
```

## tracking

Log after each session in [[training_log]] (or via `/skating-coach log`):
- Hold durations achieved
- Supported vs unsupported
- RPE (rate of perceived exertion, 1-10)
- Any form breakdown (knee bending, weight on ankle)

RPE trending down at same duration = getting stronger. Time to progress. See
[[coaching_principles]] for the progress / hold / back-off triggers.
